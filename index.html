The roast, start to finish

What actually happens in the drum

A real sequence — each stage sets up the next, and skipping one shows up in the cup.

room → 160°C

Drying phase

Green beans lose surface moisture. No color change yet — this stage decides how evenly everything after it goes.

160° → 196°C

Maillard

Sugars and amino acids react. Beans turn from green to tan to light brown — this is where flavor starts building.

196° → 205°C

First crack

An audible pop as internal pressure releases. From here, every extra minute trades acidity for body.

205°C → drop

Development

We pull most lots seconds after first crack. A few go to second crack — never past it.
